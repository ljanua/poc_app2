VantagIQ public landing — coaches & parents

Date: 2026-07-18 Topic: vantagiq-public-landing Mode: repo-grounded Focus: appealing public page; free-tier signup + sign-in; player development + AI assessment

Grounding context

Codebase: Phase 1 HTML mockups (docs/ux/mockup/) served by scripts/serve-mockup.js. Dark athletic design (Sora/Inter, lime #c6ff3a, cyan/violet). Auth today is login-only (S0-login.html) — users are provisioned by admins; no public signup. Roles: SystemAdmin, Coach, ClubAdmin. Players are roster records, not login personas. Guest share (?share= on S2/S6) is the only parent-facing path today. index.html is an internal mockup hub, not marketing.

Past learnings: Thin on landing/onboarding; coach UX learnings stress live-backend truthfulness over localStorage-only demos.

External: Youth sports conversion in 2026 favors mobile-first heroes that answer who/what/next in seconds, sticky CTAs, short forms, social proof near buttons, and seamless handoff into the product (camp/tryout landing patterns).

Topic axes

  • Hero & first-viewport promise
  • Dual audience (coach vs parent) without muddle
  • Free-tier signup & trust friction
  • Product proof (AI assessment, progress, share)
  • Handoff into existing app (login, guest share, roles)

Ranked ideas

Jump: 1. Dual-door hero 2. Parent share-first 3. Live product strip 4. Free-tier honesty 5. Role mosaic 6. Brand-matched landing shell 7. Soft athlete promise

1. Dual-door hero: “I’m a coach” / “I’m a parent”

axis: dual audience confidence: 88% complexity: Medium

First viewport is brand-forward (logo as hero signal) with one short outcome line, then two equal CTAs that route into different onboarding stories — coach free signup vs parent path (share/invite/parent signup later). Secondary “Already registered? Sign in” links to existing S0.

Basis: direct: S0 is sign-in only; no Parent role yet; guest share is parent wedge. external: youth sports pages win when audience + next step are clear in ~10s.

Rationale: Avoids a generic “Sign up free” that forces one persona into the wrong funnel while still satisfying dual CTA ask.

Downsides: Needs careful visual hierarchy so the page doesn’t feel like a dashboard of doors; parent path must not over-promise accounts that don’t exist yet.

2. Parent share-first funnel (zero account until value)

axis: free-tier / handoff confidence: 84% complexity: Low–Medium

Public page sells “see your player’s progress” and primary parent CTA is “Open a shared player link” or “Ask your coach for a VantagIQ share” — with a secondary “Create free coach account.” Reuses Feature 034 guest share as the true free tier for families.

Basis: direct: guest S2/S6 share already works read-only. reasoned: free tier for parents can be share-mediated until Parent role ships.

Rationale: Ships value without inventing Parent auth on day one; coaches become the growth loop.

Downsides: Weaker “self-serve parent signup” story; relies on coaches generating shares.

3. Live product proof strip (not stock kids-sports collage)

axis: product proof confidence: 82% complexity: Medium

Below the hero, a single full-bleed or edge-to-edge sequence of real mockup surfaces (S1 list → S2 progress → S4/S6 AI clip assessment → S10 games) with one caption each. Motion: subtle cross-fade or scroll-scrub of 2–3 screens. No fake stat tiles in the hero.

Basis: direct: rich coach journey already in mockups; design system already youth-sports energetic. external: trust comes from real product imagery near CTAs.

Rationale: Differentiates from generic camp templates; proves AI assessment claim with existing UI.

Downsides: Needs curated screenshots/assets; stale if UI drifts.

4. Honest free-tier card + Sign in twin

axis: free-tier signup confidence: 80% complexity: Medium–High

A dedicated “Start free” block that states what’s included (e.g. 1 team / N players / AI clip assessments cap) and what requires club upgrade — then email/password signup creating a Coach (or ClubAdmin-lite) account. Adjacent “Sign in” for registered users → S0. Sticky mobile CTA bar mirrors both.

Basis: direct: user asked for free-tier signup + sign-on. external: short forms + visible sticky CTA for parent/coach mobile traffic.

Rationale: Makes the commercial promise concrete; forces product to define free-tier limits early.

Downsides: Requires net-new signup API + role seeding (not in app today); needs product decisions on limits.

5. Four-persona mosaic (tools for everyone) with one primary CTA

axis: dual audience / product proof confidence: 76% complexity: Low

One section, four quiet columns — Coach, Club Admin, Parent, Athlete — each one sentence. Only one primary free CTA on the page (coach-led); parent/athlete rows say “via coach share” or “coming soon” so the mosaic matches the user brief without pretending all four can register today.

Basis: direct: user named four audiences; only three staff roles exist. reasoned: listing four builds brand aspiration while labeling honesty avoids trust break.

Rationale: Captures positioning language without lying about auth.

Downsides: Easy to over-design into a feature grid; keep one job per section.

6. Brand-matched public shell (reuse design tokens, not index.html)

axis: hero / handoff confidence: 85% complexity: Low

New public HTML (e.g. docs/ux/mockup/public-home.html or a served root route) using existing site.css variables, logo, Sora/Inter — full-bleed athletic atmosphere, brand as hero-level signal, no internal “Mockup Flow” chrome. Sign-in deep-links to S0; free signup to a new S0b or modal.

Basis: direct: index.html is prototype hub; site.css already encodes brand. Frontend design rules: brand first, one composition, avoid purple-AI cliché (product already lime/cyan athletic).

Rationale: Fast path to an appealing page that feels like the product, not a separate WordPress skin.

Downsides: Must not ship the internal mockup index as marketing.

7. Soft athlete promise without athlete login

axis: dual audience confidence: 72% complexity: Low

Copy line for athletes: “Athletes grow through coach-led assessments and shared progress — athlete accounts coming.” Keeps the four-audience narrative while avoiding a dead signup door.

Basis: direct: no Athlete role. reasoned: young athletes rarely self-provision; parents/coaches mediate.

Rationale: Prevents scope overrun into athlete auth while honoring the brief.

Downsides: Some visitors may bounce if they wanted athlete-first TikTok-style signup.
